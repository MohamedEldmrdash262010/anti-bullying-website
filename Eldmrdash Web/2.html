What is Bullying?

Bullying is a deliberate and repeated behavior intended to harm or intimidate another person. It involves an imbalance of power, where one individual or group uses their advantage to control or inflict pain on someone else. Bullying can take many forms, including:

  • Physical aggression (such as hitting or pushing)
  • Verbal abuse (such as name-calling or threats)
  • Social manipulation (such as spreading rumors or excluding someone from a group)

The core elements of bullying are its intent to cause harm and the power imbalance between the bully and the victim. It can occur in various settings, including schools, workplaces, and online platforms. The impact of bullying extends beyond immediate physical harm, often leading to emotional distress, low self-esteem, and long-term psychological issues for the victim.

Understanding and addressing bullying involves recognizing its various forms, promoting awareness, and implementing effective strategies to prevent and respond to these harmful behaviors. Creating a culture of respect and empathy is crucial in combating bullying and supporting those affected.
